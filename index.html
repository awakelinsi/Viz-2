THE WORLD AND ITS DRUG PROBLEM

-a visualization about the generational global problem the world has against illicit drugs-

UNODC Executive Director stated as illicit-drug problems continue to hit record highs even during a time of global chaos, misperceptions around the severity of the problem and the associated risks are depriving people of care and treatment affecting generations to come.

2019 Global Drug Mortality

The estimated number of lives lost directly caused by illicit drugs in 2019 is around 128,000. The choropleth map shows normalized death rates per 100,000 population correlating to the color range. As seen from the map, United States holds the largest drug mortality rate in 2019.

Drug Mortality Based on Region?

Looking back to historical 10 year data, America has continuously shown significantly higher numbers of drug mortality. The heatmap below also verifies the severity of drug mortality in America, with 2.11% of North America's death and 0.98% of South America's death caused by drugs.

Direct Causation and Indirect Causation?

TWO out of TEN deaths are caused directly by drugs while the others are caused by the risk factors. Indirect deaths are the result from illicit drug use acting as risk factors for diseases.
The bubble plot below visualizes each country based on the number of direct drug mortality cases and indirect drug mortality cases. The size of the bubble correlates to the population size of the country. Since the data is not normalized, it is expected that countries with larger populations will place higher. However, United States is at the top of the line with a notably smaller population size amongst countries near such as China.

The largest age group with the most people associated with drug prevalence is the 20-24 and 25-29 years old age bracket which remains consistent over the past 20 years. This fact highlights that we are going to face drug mortality both directly and indirectly for generations to come.

Substance Type Contribution?

Over the last 20 years, the donut chart has shown that opioid is the largest contributor of drug mortality by a very large margin compared to other substance types. As seen from the faceted group chart, drug deaths by opioid continue to dominate over the last 5 years of data almost reaching 90,000 deaths.
